What is the Dead Stock Calculator?

Dead stock is inventory that has not sold in 90+ days and is unlikely to sell at full price. It silently destroys working capital — tying up cash, occupying warehouse space, and accumulating holding cost every month. Most businesses discover dead stock late because it doesn't trigger any alert in basic inventory systems.

This calculator quantifies the true financial damage from dead stock and models three recovery options: clearance sale (fastest cash), liquidation (bulk sale to a reseller), and the ongoing cost of holding it longer. The result almost always shows that acting fast is less costly than waiting.

Frequently Asked Questions
What qualifies as dead stock?
Industry standard: inventory with zero sales in the last 90 days is classified as slow-moving, and 180+ days without sales is dead stock. For fashion and electronics, use 60 days as the threshold given rapid trend and technology changes. Your ABC analysis will typically surface C-class SKUs with very low sales velocity as the primary dead stock candidates.
Is liquidation ever better than clearance?
Only when you need cash urgently (liquidity crisis) or when the product has become completely unsaleable at any consumer price (expired products, discontinued items). In most other cases, a clearance sale — even at 40–50% off — generates 2–4x more recovery value than liquidation at 15–25% of cost. The key is acting early enough that clearance is still an option.
Can I write off dead stock for tax purposes?
Yes. Under Indian Income Tax, you can claim a deduction for inventory written off as worthless or below market value. Ensure you have documentation: stock verification report, ageing analysis, and evidence that the stock was offered for sale at reduced prices. Consult your CA before writing off significant inventory values. Under GST, a write-off may also require reversal of ITC claimed on the purchase.
How do I identify dead stock in Tally or SAP?
In Tally Prime: Gateway of Tally → Stock Summary → filter by 'No Movement' or run the 'Movement Analysis' report with a 90-day no-movement filter. In SAP: Transaction MC50 (Slow-Moving Analysis) or MB52 (Warehouse Stocks) filtered by last movement date. Amazon Seller Central: Inventory → Manage Inventory → filter by 'Slow-moving' in the Health dashboard.
