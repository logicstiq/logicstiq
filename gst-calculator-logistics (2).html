What is Freight Cost Calculator?

Freight cost is the charge for transporting goods from one location to another. In India, it varies significantly by carrier, transport mode (road/air/rail), weight, distance, and the type of goods being shipped.

For D2C brands and marketplace sellers, freight cost is often the second-largest cost after product cost. A 10% reduction in freight cost can significantly improve net margins — especially for sellers shipping hundreds or thousands of orders per month.

The Freight Cost Formula

📐 Formula
Freight Cost = MAX(Weight × Rate per Kg, Minimum Charge)

Most Indian carriers charge either a per-kg rate or a minimum charge — whichever is higher. For courier services, volumetric weight (L×W×H÷5000) may apply if it exceeds actual weight. Always compare both to find the true applicable charge.

Worked Example

A seller in Mumbai ships a 5kg parcel to Delhi (1,400 km) via a courier at ₹40/kg with a ₹150 minimum charge.

📊 Example Calculation
Shipment weight5 kg
Rate per kg₹40
Weight-based charge5 × ₹40 = ₹200
Minimum charge₹150
Applicable chargeMAX(₹200, ₹150)
Freight Cost₹200

The weight-based charge (₹200) exceeds the minimum charge (₹150), so ₹200 applies. If the shipment were only 3kg, the minimum charge of ₹150 would apply instead.

Why Freight Cost Matters for Your Business

Freight cost directly impacts product margins and customer pricing. Small improvements in freight cost compound significantly at scale.

  • Carrier selection: Same shipment can vary ₹50-200 across carriers
  • Weight consolidation: Combining small shipments into one larger one often reduces per-unit cost
  • Zone optimisation: Locating inventory closer to high-demand regions reduces freight zones and costs
  • Contract rates: Volume commitments with carriers can reduce rates 15-30%

Frequently Asked Questions

How do Indian courier companies calculate freight charges?▾
Indian courier companies charge based on actual weight or volumetric weight (L×W×H in cm ÷ 5000), whichever is higher, subject to a minimum charge. Delhivery, Shiprocket, Blue Dart, and XpressBees all use this structure with varying per-kg rates by zone and service level.
What is the GST rate on courier services in India?▾
Courier services (Shiprocket, Delhivery, Blue Dart, DTDC, XpressBees) attract 18% GST on the freight charge. This is under SAC code 9968. If you are a GST-registered business, you can claim ITC on this 18% GST against your output tax liability.
How can I reduce freight costs for my e-commerce business?▾
Key strategies: (1) Negotiate volume-based contracts with 2-3 preferred carriers, (2) Use a multi-carrier platform like Shiprocket that auto-selects cheapest carrier per shipment, (3) Reduce average shipment weight through lighter packaging, (4) Establish regional fulfilment points to reduce shipping zones, (5) Use surface transport for heavy, non-urgent B2B shipments.
