What is Landed Cost Calculator?

Landed Cost is the total cost of a product from the supplier's location to your warehouse or fulfillment centre. It includes the purchase price plus all freight, customs duties, taxes, insurance, handling, and any other charges incurred along the way.

For Indian importers and domestic buyers, landed cost is critical for accurate product costing, margin calculation, and supplier comparison. A product that looks cheaper from Supplier A may actually cost more once freight, GST (non-recoverable portion), and handling are included.

The Landed Cost Formula

📐 Formula
Landed Cost per Unit = (Purchase Price × Qty + Freight + Duties + GST + Other) ÷ Quantity

Landed cost includes every cost incurred to get a product from your supplier to your warehouse — not just the invoice price. Businesses that price products based on purchase price alone systematically underestimate costs and erode margins without knowing why.

Worked Example

An electronics importer brings in 500 units of wireless earbuds from a supplier at ₹800/unit. Let's calculate the true landed cost.

📊 Example Calculation
Purchase price₹800 × 500 = ₹4,00,000
Sea freight (Mumbai port)₹25,000
Customs duty (20% on assessable value)₹80,000
IGST (12% on assessable + duty)₹57,600
Clearing agent + handling₹8,000
Total landed cost₹5,70,600
Landed cost per unit₹5,70,600 ÷ 500 = ₹1,141.20

The true landed cost is ₹1,141 per unit — 42.6% higher than the invoice price of ₹800. Any business pricing at a 30% markup over ₹800 would actually be selling at a loss once all costs are included.

Why Landed Cost Matters for Your Business

Landed cost is the single most important number for accurate product pricing and supplier comparison. Ignoring it leads to the most common profitability mistake in supply chain — calculating margins on purchase price instead of true cost.

  • Accurate pricing: Set selling prices that cover all costs and deliver the target margin
  • Fair supplier comparison: Compare two suppliers on landed cost, not just invoice price
  • Import planning: Understand the true cost of importing vs. buying domestically
  • GST planning: Identify which portions of GST are recoverable as ITC and which add to your actual cost

Frequently Asked Questions

Does landed cost include GST?▾
It depends. If you are a GST-registered business and the GST is recoverable as Input Tax Credit (ITC), then GST should NOT be included in landed cost (it is effectively zero cost to you). If GST is not recoverable — for example, RCM on GTA services, or if you are not GST registered — then include it as part of your landed cost. Our calculator includes a GST field for the non-recoverable portion only.
How do I calculate landed cost for Amazon FBA imports?▾
For Amazon FBA imports, your landed cost includes: product price + international freight (air or sea) + customs duty + IGST (may be recoverable if you are GST registered) + customs clearance agent fees + domestic freight to FBA warehouse + FBA receiving fees. Many Amazon sellers calculate landed cost excluding recoverable IGST and including FBA fees to get true cost before Amazon commission.
Should I include Amazon or Flipkart commission in landed cost?▾
No. Amazon and Flipkart commissions (typically 5-15%) are selling costs, not product costs. Calculate landed cost first (true cost to get product to your warehouse), then factor in platform commission separately when calculating your net margin per unit. Mixing the two makes pricing decisions confusing.
