What is Inventory Carrying Cost Calculator?

Inventory carrying cost (also called holding cost) is the total annual cost of maintaining inventory in your warehouse. It includes direct costs like storage and insurance, and indirect costs like the opportunity cost of capital tied up in stock.

The most commonly underestimated component is cost of capital. If your inventory is funded by a bank loan at 12% interest, or if that cash could earn 12% invested elsewhere, then holding ₹20L of inventory costs ₹2.4L/year in capital cost alone — before adding warehouse rent, insurance, and shrinkage.

The Carrying Cost Formula

📐 Formula
Carrying Cost = Average Inventory Value × Carrying Cost Rate %

The carrying cost rate is the sum of all individual holding cost components expressed as a percentage of inventory value. Typical range: 18-35% annually. Most Indian SMEs underestimate this because they only count warehouse rent and ignore the cost of capital tied up in stock.

Worked Example

A FMCG distributor holds an average inventory of ₹25 lakh. Their carrying cost components:

Insurance1% × ₹25L = ₹25,000

This distributor spends ₹6 lakh per year just to hold their inventory — ₹50,000 per month. Every ₹1 lakh reduction in average inventory saves ₹24,000 annually. This is why EOQ and safety stock optimisation have direct, measurable financial returns.

Why Carrying Cost Matters for Your Business

Carrying cost is the "hidden tax" on inventory. Most businesses see the cost of buying inventory but not the cost of holding it — leading to systematic over-ordering.

  • EOQ optimisation: Carrying cost is a direct input into the EOQ formula — accurate carrying cost gives accurate EOQ
  • Dead stock decisions: Compare the carrying cost of holding slow stock vs. the cost of clearing it
  • Working capital management: Justify inventory reduction initiatives to management using annual carrying cost savings
  • Safety stock calibration: Balance the cost of carrying safety stock against the cost of a stockout

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a typical carrying cost percentage for Indian businesses?▾
For Indian businesses, total carrying cost typically ranges from 18% to 30% of inventory value annually. Breakdown: Cost of capital 10-14% (bank OD rates), Warehouse/storage 5-10%, Insurance 0.5-1.5%, Obsolescence/shrinkage 2-5%. FMCG and fashion tend to have higher obsolescence costs. Industrial components tend to have lower shrinkage.
How does carrying cost affect the EOQ calculation?▾
Carrying cost is one of the two key inputs in the EOQ formula: EOQ = √(2DS/H) where H is the annual holding cost per unit. A higher carrying cost percentage results in a lower EOQ (smaller, more frequent orders) because the cost of holding a large order outweighs the ordering savings. Accurate carrying cost is essential for meaningful EOQ calculation.
Should I include depreciation in carrying cost?▾
Yes, for physical goods that lose value over time — electronics, fashion, food, chemicals — include depreciation or obsolescence as a percentage of inventory value. For stable goods like industrial components or construction materials, obsolescence may be low (1-2%). For fast-changing categories like mobile phones, it can be 10-15% or more.
