How an Amazon FBA seller plans restock before Big Billion Days

A worked example of peak-season FBA replenishment — how much to send into Amazon's fulfilment centres, when to ship it, and how to stay inside restock limits.

ℹ️ Illustrative worked example — the seller and numbers are representative, not a real named customer. Amazon restock limits, IPI thresholds and fees change; always confirm current limits in Seller Central before shipping.

The situation

An FBA seller of a hero kitchen gadget normally sells ~20 units/day. Prime Day and the Great Indian Festival are weeks away, and FBA adds two twists a marketplace-only seller doesn't face: an inbound lead time to get stock checked-in at the FC, and a restock limit that caps how much they're allowed to send. The goal: send enough to ride the peak, ship early enough to be checked in, and not get blocked by the limit.

Step 1 — Forecast the peak-window demand

Baseline 20 units/day, a 3.5× festive uplift over a 12-day peak window:

Festive daily demand = 20 × 3.5 = 70 units/day Peak-window demand = 70 × 12 = 840 units

Step 2 — Ship early for the FC inbound lead time

FBA check-in isn't instant — allow ~10–14 days from dispatch to sellable, and longer near peak. Working back from a sale that starts in 25 days, the shipment must leave now to be live in time. Late = your units sit in "inbound" while the sale runs.

Step 3 — Add safety stock, then check the restock limit

Buffer for the spike (95% service, σd 18 units, 12-day lead):

Safety stock = 1.65 × 18 × √12 ≈ 103 units Send-in target = 840 + 103 − on-hand at FC (250) = ~693 units

Now confirm your restock limit allows ~693 units. If the limit is tighter, prioritise this hero SKU's allocation and trim slow movers from the shipment.

Step 4 — Keep a self-ship (FBM) buffer

Hold ~15–20% of peak stock in your own warehouse as FBM backup. If FBA sells through or check-in is delayed, you switch that SKU to merchant-fulfilled and keep selling instead of going dark mid-sale.

✅ The outcome

The seller ships ~693 units into FBA well ahead of check-in, keeps a self-ship buffer for safety, and confirms the restock limit covers it. They enter Prime Day / GIF fully sellable, avoid the "inbound-but-not-live" trap, and don't over-send stock that would later rack up long-term-storage fees.
