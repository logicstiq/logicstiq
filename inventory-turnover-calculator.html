The Formula

📐 Inventory Turnover Ratio
Turnover = COGS ÷ Average Inventory | DIO = 365 ÷ Turnover Ratio

Average Inventory = (Opening Stock + Closing Stock) ÷ 2. Use the COGS method (not revenue) for accurate results that compare fairly with industry benchmarks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good inventory turnover ratio?▾
It depends entirely on your industry. FMCG targets 15–25x. Beauty 6–12x. Fashion 4–6x. Furniture 2–4x. Always benchmark within your specific category — a 4x ratio is excellent for furniture and dangerously low for groceries.
Should I use COGS or revenue for the calculation?▾
Use COGS. It compares inventory at cost to sales at cost. Revenue includes your profit margin which inflates the ratio. Most industry benchmarks use the COGS method. If your Tally or ERP only shows sales value, divide by your average gross margin to estimate COGS.
How do I improve my inventory turnover?▾
To increase turnover (too much stock): improve demand forecasting to reduce over-ordering, run ABC analysis and reduce safety stock on C items, identify and liquidate dead stock, reduce reorder quantities using EOQ. To decrease turnover (too little stock, frequent stockouts): increase safety stock on fast-moving SKUs, add safety stock buffers for A items.
