What is Logistics Carbon Footprint Calculator?

Logistics carbon footprint is the total greenhouse gas emissions generated by transporting your goods — measured in kg of CO2 equivalent (CO2e). It is a key component of Scope 3 emissions under the GHG Protocol.

For Indian exporters, large brands, and businesses with sustainability commitments, calculating logistics carbon footprint is becoming mandatory. The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) and ESG reporting requirements from investors mean this is no longer optional for businesses with global supply chains.

The Carbon Footprint Formula

📐 Formula
CO2 (kg) = Weight (tonnes) × Distance (km) × Emission Factor

Emission factors (kg CO2 per tonne-km): Road truck: 0.096 | Rail: 0.028 | Sea (container): 0.016 | Air freight: 0.602. Air freight produces 37x more CO2 than sea freight per tonne-km. Road produces 6x more than rail.

Worked Example

A Mumbai exporter ships 2 tonnes of textiles to Delhi by road truck (1,400 km) vs the same shipment by rail.

📊 Example Calculation
Shipment weight2 tonnes
Distance1,400 km
Road truck emission factor0.096 kg CO2/tonne-km
Road CO22 × 1,400 × 0.096 = 268.8 kg CO2
Rail emission factor0.028 kg CO2/tonne-km
Rail CO22 × 1,400 × 0.028 = 78.4 kg CO2

Shifting this shipment from road to rail reduces emissions by 190.4 kg CO2 — a 71% reduction. Over 100 such shipments per year, that is 19 tonnes of CO2 avoided — roughly equivalent to planting 950 trees.

Why Carbon Footprint Matters for Your Business

Carbon footprint calculation is becoming a business requirement, not just a sustainability exercise. Multiple forces are making this data essential.

  • EU CBAM compliance: Mandatory carbon reporting for goods exported to the EU
  • ESG investor reporting: Scope 3 supply chain emissions required by SEBI for listed companies
  • Customer requirements: Large brands now require suppliers to disclose and reduce logistics emissions
  • Cost correlation: Lower emissions routes often correlate with lower freight costs

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Scope 3 emissions in supply chain?▾
Scope 3 emissions are indirect greenhouse gas emissions from activities outside your direct operations — including the transportation of your goods (upstream and downstream logistics). Under the GHG Protocol, logistics emissions fall under Scope 3 Category 4 (upstream transportation) and Category 9 (downstream transportation). Many large Indian companies are now required to disclose Scope 3 emissions.
Which transport mode has the lowest carbon footprint?▾
In order from lowest to highest CO2 per tonne-km: Sea freight (0.016) → Rail (0.028) → Road truck (0.096) → Air freight (0.602). Sea freight is the most environmentally efficient for long distances. Rail is best for inland long-distance freight. Air freight has the highest emissions by far — approximately 37x more than sea per tonne-km.
How do I reduce my logistics carbon footprint?▾
Key strategies: (1) Shift from road to rail for long-distance inland shipments, (2) Optimise load factor — consolidate shipments to fill trucks more efficiently, (3) Reduce air freight by improving demand forecasting and lead time planning, (4) Work with carriers who use Euro 6 / BS6 vehicles, (5) Choose shorter supply chains by sourcing closer to your market.
