What is OTIF?

OTIF (On-Time In-Full) is the most important delivery performance metric in supply chain. An order qualifies as OTIF only when it satisfies two conditions simultaneously: delivered on or before the agreed date AND shipped with 100% of the requested quantity.

One condition alone is not enough. An order that arrives on time but is short by 2 units fails OTIF. An order with complete quantity that arrives one day late also fails OTIF. Both must be met on every order.

The OTIF Formula

📐 OTIF Formula
OTIF % = (Orders meeting BOTH On-Time AND In-Full ÷ Total Orders) × 100

Count only orders that satisfy both conditions together — not orders that satisfy each condition independently. An 95% on-time rate and 95% in-full rate does not mean 95% OTIF. The combined OTIF is approximately 90.25% (0.95 × 0.95) because some orders fail both conditions.

How to Improve Your OTIF Score

  • Fix demand forecasting first: Most In-Full failures come from stockouts caused by poor demand planning. Accurate forecasts prevent the inventory gaps that cause short shipments.
  • Increase safety stock for key accounts: For accounts where OTIF penalties apply, holding higher buffer stock for their specific SKUs costs far less than the penalty.
  • Build carrier scorecards: Identify which carriers are causing On-Time failures and route time-sensitive orders through higher-performing carriers.
  • Set conservative delivery commitments: Build buffer time into committed delivery dates — underpromise and overdeliver.
  • Track OTIF by SKU: An 88% overall OTIF often hides 5–10 specific SKUs with 60% OTIF. Fix the worst performers first.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good OTIF score?▾
For most Indian supply chains, 95%+ is excellent. Walmart requires 98%+ from suppliers. Amazon Vendor Central expects 93%+. Below 90% triggers penalties from most large retailers. For automotive and pharma, targets are even higher at 98–99%+.
How is OTIF different from on-time delivery rate?▾
On-time delivery only checks whether orders arrived on schedule. OTIF requires BOTH on-time AND in-full (100% quantity shipped). An order arriving on time but missing 2 units fails OTIF even though it passes the on-time delivery metric. OTIF is a stricter, more complete measure of supply chain performance.
What penalties do Walmart and Amazon charge for low OTIF?▾
Walmart charges a financial penalty of 3% of the cost of goods for OTIF below 98%. Amazon Vendor Central charges chargebacks for Receive Fill Rate below 95% and may reduce purchase order volumes or remove vendor status for persistently low OTIF. Indian modern trade retailers like Reliance Retail and D-Mart typically charge 1–2% invoice deductions for OTIF below 90%.
