What is Cycle Count Calculator?

Cycle counting is the practice of counting a small subset of your inventory on a rotating schedule throughout the year, rather than conducting one large annual physical inventory count that requires shutting down operations.

Cycle counting is superior to annual counts for three reasons: inventory accuracy is maintained year-round rather than drifting for 11 months, counting disruption is spread out across the year instead of concentrated in one shutdown, and discrepancies are caught quickly when they are smaller and easier to investigate.

The Cycle Count Formula

📐 Formula
Daily Count = [(A SKUs × A Frequency) + (B SKUs × B Frequency) + (C SKUs × C Frequency)] ÷ Working Days

Different ABC categories require different counting frequencies. A-items (high-value) should be counted most frequently (monthly or quarterly). B-items quarterly. C-items annually. This focuses counting effort where inventory accuracy matters most.

Worked Example

A warehouse has 600 SKUs split across ABC categories. They operate 250 days per year with A items counted monthly.

SKUs to count per day1,600 ÷ 250 = 6.4 → 7 SKUs/day

With a target of 7 SKUs per day, this warehouse maintains full cycle count coverage with minimal daily effort. An experienced counter can typically count 20-50 SKUs per hour, so this represents only 15-25 minutes of counting per day.

Why Cycle Count Matters for Your Business

Inventory accuracy above 95% is a prerequisite for reliable demand planning, order fulfilment, and financial reporting. Cycle counting is the most cost-effective way to maintain this accuracy.

  • No shutdown required: Operations continue normally while counting proceeds on a rolling schedule
  • Faster discrepancy detection: Errors found within days, not months — easier to investigate
  • ABC-weighted effort: High-value A items get the most attention
  • GSTR compliance: Accurate inventory records support GST filing and audit defence

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between cycle counting and physical inventory?▾
A physical inventory count counts every SKU in the warehouse at one time, typically annually, requiring a warehouse shutdown. Cycle counting counts a portion of SKUs every day on a rotating schedule, requiring no shutdown. Cycle counting is preferred for warehouses with more than 100 SKUs because it maintains continuous accuracy rather than point-in-time accuracy.
How accurate should my inventory be?▾
Industry best practice targets 98%+ inventory accuracy (measured as the percentage of SKUs with quantity matching the system record exactly). Below 95% causes significant operational problems: stockouts when system shows available, wrong reorder signals, customer fulfilment errors. Cycle counting is the primary tool for achieving and maintaining 98%+ accuracy.
How many times should I count A, B, and C items per year?▾
Standard practice: A items 12 times/year (monthly), B items 4 times/year (quarterly), C items 1-2 times/year (annually). Some high-accuracy operations count A items weekly (52x). The frequency should reflect how much damage an inventory error on that item would cause — high-value, fast-moving items need the most frequent verification.
