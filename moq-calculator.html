What is the MOQ Calculator?

A Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) is the smallest number of units a supplier will sell in one order. Suppliers set MOQs to cover their production setup costs and ensure orders are profitable for them. For buyers, MOQs create a trade-off: lower unit cost in exchange for buying more stock than you immediately need.

This calculator quantifies that trade-off precisely — comparing the unit price savings from the MOQ against the extra holding cost of carrying months of excess stock. If savings exceed holding cost, the MOQ is financially viable. If not, the alternative supplier or a smaller order makes more sense.

Why This Calculator Matters

Avoid the MOQ Trap

Buying 6 months of stock to save ₹5 per unit often costs more in holding cost than the saving. This calculator quantifies that trade-off before you commit.

Supplier Negotiation

Use the analysis to negotiate. Show the supplier that their MOQ creates ₹X in holding cost for you — and ask for a lower MOQ or a better price at a smaller quantity.

Cash Flow Planning

MOQ orders lock up working capital for months. Knowing the payback period helps decide whether to finance the order via bank OD or negotiate extended payment terms.

India Sourcing Context

Common in Indian manufacturing — textiles (Surat, Tiruppur), electronics (Shenzhen imports via JNPT), and FMCG packaging. MOQ analysis is essential before every bulk purchase order.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the difference between MOQ and EOQ?
MOQ is set by the supplier — the minimum they will sell you. EOQ is calculated by you — the optimal order quantity that minimises your total inventory cost (ordering + holding). A smart buying strategy considers both: start with EOQ to understand your optimal quantity, then negotiate with the supplier to get as close to EOQ as possible within their MOQ constraints.
What should I include in monthly holding cost per unit?
Monthly holding cost per unit = (warehouse rent allocation + insurance + capital cost) ÷ average units stored. A rough rule of thumb: 2% of unit cost per month for ambient storage in India (24% annually). For cold storage, 3–4% per month. Include the cost of capital — if you are financing inventory via bank OD at 12% annually, that is 1% per month of the unit cost.
What if I cannot meet the MOQ alone?
Consider collaborative buying — pooling your MOQ requirement with a complementary business (different geography or different customer base). This is common in the Indian FMCG distribution network. Some platforms like IndiaMart and TradeIndia facilitate group buying from manufacturers.
How do I negotiate a lower MOQ with an Indian supplier?
Share your volume projection for the next 12 months. Offer longer payment terms or advance payment in exchange for a lower MOQ. Agree to a pilot order at a slightly higher per-unit price — prove the demand, then commit to larger quantities. Most Indian manufacturers are willing to negotiate MOQ for reliable buyers.
