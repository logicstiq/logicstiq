What is Reorder Point Calculator?

The Reorder Point (ROP) is the inventory level at which you must place a new purchase order to avoid a stockout. It accounts for the time it takes your supplier to deliver (lead time) and your daily demand during that period, plus a safety buffer for variability.

Without a clearly defined reorder point, supply chain teams either order too early (excess inventory, high carrying costs) or too late (stockouts, lost sales, customer penalties). The reorder point eliminates guesswork by giving you a precise trigger level backed by your actual demand and lead time data.

The ROP Formula

📐 Formula
ROP = (Average Daily Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock

The reorder point formula multiplies your average daily demand by your supplier lead time, then adds your safety stock buffer. The result is the inventory level at which you must place a new order — giving you enough stock to last through the replenishment period without running out.

Worked Example

A D2C brand in Delhi sells 80 units of their bestselling product per day. Their supplier in Surat takes 5 days to deliver. They maintain 200 units of safety stock.

📊 Example Calculation
Average daily demand80 units/day
Supplier lead time5 days
Safety stock200 units
Demand during lead time80 × 5 = 400 units
Reorder Point400 + 200 = 600 units

This means: when inventory drops to 600 units, place the next purchase order immediately. By the time it arrives 5 days later, you will have consumed approximately 400 units and still have 200 units of safety stock remaining.

Why ROP Matters for Your Business

The reorder point is one of the most operationally critical numbers in any supply chain. Without it, ordering decisions are made on gut feel — leading to either excess stock or stockouts, both of which cost money.

  • Prevents stockouts: You always know when to act before running out
  • Reduces excess inventory: You order when needed, not based on guesswork
  • Automates the trigger: Set ROP as an alert in your ERP or WMS
  • Works with any product: FMCG, electronics, fashion, industrial components
  • Links to EOQ: Use ROP to trigger an EOQ-sized order for optimal replenishment

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between reorder point and reorder level?▾
Reorder point and reorder level mean the same thing — both refer to the inventory quantity at which a new purchase order should be placed. Some systems use "reorder level" (common in Indian ERPs like Tally and Busy) while others use "reorder point" (common in SAP and NetSuite).
How often should I recalculate my reorder point?▾
Recalculate your reorder point whenever demand changes significantly (seasonal spikes, promotions, new distribution channels), when your supplier lead time changes, or when you update your safety stock policy. For fast-moving products, monthly review is recommended. For stable products, quarterly is sufficient.
Can I use this calculator for Amazon FBA inventory?▾
Yes. For Amazon FBA, your lead time should include manufacturing time plus shipping to the FBA warehouse plus Amazon processing time (typically 2-5 days). Your daily demand should be your average daily units sold from Seller Central reports. This gives you the inventory level at which to ship your next FBA replenishment.
