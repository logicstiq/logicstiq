OMS — Order Management System

Every sale you make — on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, a quick-commerce platform or your own D2C store — becomes an order that must be confirmed, packed, invoiced, dispatched and delivered inside a strict SLA. Order management is the discipline of running that pipeline across all channels at once, and it is where multi-channel sellers most often lose money and ratings.

⚙️ How order management works

Order capture

Orders arrive continuously from every channel, each with its own portal, formats and SLA clocks. Without a single view, sellers tab-hop between five dashboards all day.

Processing & dispatch

Each order moves through confirm → pack → invoice → manifest → handover. Miss a step or a cutoff time and the marketplace penalises your seller metrics.

The stock connection

Every order must decrement the right stock pool. When channels sell from the same inventory without coordination, overselling and cancellations follow.

🛠️ What our OMS will do

One inbox for all orders

Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, quick-commerce and D2C orders in a single queue with unified statuses and SLA countdowns.

Dispatch readiness

A packing queue that shows exactly what must ship today, in cutoff order, so nothing breaches SLA.

Channel-aware stock view

See which channel each unit is committed to, and get alerts before overselling happens.

🎯 Problems we are solving

No single view

Sellers juggle multiple seller panels with different rules — our OMS gives one operational picture.

SLA breaches & penalties

Missed cutoffs quietly damage account health; deadline-ordered queues prevent that.

Overselling & cancellations

Cancelled orders hurt ratings more than slow ones; a coordinated stock view stops selling what you do not have.
