How a Flipkart & Amazon seller plans inventory for Big Billion Days

A step-by-step walkthrough of festive-sale inventory planning for an Indian marketplace seller — from baseline demand to festive uplift, safety stock and reorder timing — using LogicstIQ's free tools.

ℹ️ This is an illustrative worked example built to show the method and the tools. The seller and numbers are representative, not a real named customer. Always validate against your own data before placing purchase orders.

The situation

Meet a mid-size seller of phone accessories (cases, chargers, TWS earbuds) selling on Flipkart and Amazon.in. Last year they under-stocked their hero SKU before Big Billion Days, went out of stock on day 2, lost the sales rank they'd built, and spent weeks recovering. This year they want to plan properly — but they don't have SAP, and Excel-plus-WhatsApp isn't cutting it.

Their goal: work out how many units of each hero SKU to order, and by when, so they survive the Big Billion Days + Great Indian Festival window without stocking out or drowning in dead stock afterward.

The plan, in four steps

Step 1 — Establish baseline demand

Start with normal, non-sale velocity. For the hero SKU (a TWS earbud), the last 30 days sold 900 units — an average of 30 units/day. That's the baseline the festive multiplier will be applied to.

Baseline daily demand = 900 units ÷ 30 days = 30 units/day

Rather than doing this SKU-by-SKU in a spreadsheet, upload your Flipkart/Amazon sales export to the AI Demand Planner — it computes per-SKU velocity, trend and 30/60/90-day forecasts automatically.

Step 2 — Apply the festive uplift

Indian festive sales don't nudge demand — they multiply it. For electronics accessories, a 4× uplift over the peak sale days is realistic. Over a 10-day peak window:

Festive daily demand = 30 units/day × 4 = 120 units/day Peak-window demand = 120 × 10 days = 1,200 units

The Demand Planner's India Festival Intelligence applies category-specific uplift automatically for Big Billion Days, Great Indian Festival, Diwali, Navratri and Myntra EORS that fall inside your horizon — so you don't have to guess the multiplier per category.

Step 3 — Add safety stock for the spike

Demand and supplier lead times both get more variable around festivals, so a buffer is essential. Using the standard formula at a 95% service level (Z = 1.65), with daily-demand variability of 25 units and a 9-day lead time:

Safety stock = Z × σd × √LT = 1.65 × 25 × √9 = 1.65 × 25 × 3 ≈ 124 units

Step 4 — Set the reorder point and order date

The reorder point tells you the stock level that should trigger your festive PO — accounting for the lead time at festive velocity, plus the safety stock:

Reorder point = (festive daily demand × lead time) + safety stock = (120 × 9) + 124 = 1,080 + 124 = 1,204 units

With a 9-day supplier lead time, the PO must be placed at least 9 days before the sale — and earlier in practice, since supplier and courier lead times stretch before Diwali. The planner flags the reorder-by date for every SKU so nothing slips.

✅ The outcome

Instead of eyeballing it, the seller now knows: order enough to cover ~1,200 peak-window units plus a 124-unit buffer, and trigger the PO when stock hits 1,204 — at least 9 days out. They enter the sale covered, hold rank through the peak, and avoid the post-festival dead-stock hangover on the SKUs they didn't over-order.
