What is Supplier Scorecard Calculator?

A Supplier Scorecard is a structured tool for evaluating and comparing supplier performance across multiple dimensions using objective, weighted criteria. It removes subjectivity from procurement decisions and gives you a consistent way to track supplier performance over time.

Most Indian businesses evaluate suppliers informally — based on relationships, last experience, or price alone. A formal scorecard enables fair comparison between multiple suppliers, identifies specific improvement areas, and provides documentation for supplier development conversations or exit decisions.

The Supplier Score Formula

📐 Formula
Supplier Score = Σ (Category Score × Category Weight %)

A weighted scorecard gives different importance levels to different performance dimensions. Delivery and quality typically have higher weights (30-40% each) because failures in these areas have the most operational impact. Price and service are important but secondary.

Worked Example

A manufacturer evaluates two packaging suppliers using a weighted scorecard:

📊 Example Calculation
CriterionSupplier A | Supplier B | Weight
Delivery Performance85 | 72 | 35%
Quality90 | 88 | 30%
Price Competitiveness70 | 82 | 20%
Responsiveness80 | 65 | 15%
Supplier A Score(85×0.35)+(90×0.30)+(70×0.20)+(80×0.15) = 82.75
Supplier B Score(72×0.35)+(88×0.30)+(82×0.20)+(65×0.15) = 77.25

Despite Supplier B having a better price score, Supplier A scores higher overall because their delivery and quality performance — the highest-weighted criteria — are significantly better. This is the value of a weighted scorecard over single-metric comparison.

Why Supplier Score Matters for Your Business

Supplier scorecards are essential for professional procurement management and are required for ISO 9001 certification, which many large Indian manufacturers and exporters maintain.

  • Objective comparison: Compare suppliers on equal terms with consistent criteria
  • Performance tracking: Run the scorecard quarterly to track supplier improvement over time
  • Supplier development: Use scores to have structured conversations about specific improvement areas
  • ISO 9001: Documented supplier evaluation is a mandatory requirement for ISO 9001 certified manufacturers

Frequently Asked Questions

What criteria should I include in a supplier scorecard?▾
Standard criteria: Delivery (on-time delivery rate, lead time consistency), Quality (defect rate, rejection rate, returns), Cost (price competitiveness, cost reduction over time), Service (responsiveness to queries, flexibility on order changes, technical support). Add industry-specific criteria as needed — e.g., sustainability/ESG for export-oriented businesses.
How often should I run supplier scorecards?▾
Quarterly is the standard for most businesses. Monthly for critical or problem suppliers where you are actively managing performance improvement. Annually for low-volume, non-critical suppliers. The key is consistency — run the scorecard on the same criteria at the same frequency to make trend comparisons meaningful.
What score should I require from suppliers?▾
Typical thresholds: 80+ = preferred supplier (long-term relationship, priority for new business), 65-79 = approved supplier (monitor and work on improvement plan), 50-64 = conditional supplier (formal improvement plan required, consider alternatives), below 50 = consider exit. Adjust thresholds based on your industry and the criticality of the supplied material.
