ABC Classification Guide

Class A (top ~70% of revenue): Your highest-value SKUs. Manage tightly — high safety stock, frequent review, best supplier SLAs, weekly monitoring.

Class B (next ~20% of revenue): Mid-value items. Standard processes — monthly review, moderate safety stock, EOQ-based ordering.

Class C (remaining ~10% of revenue): Low-value items. Simplified management — quarterly review, minimal safety stock, consider rationalising the range.

Frequently Asked Questions

What percentage of SKUs should be in category A?▾
Typically 10–20% of SKUs fall into Class A, generating 70–80% of revenue. This reflects the Pareto Principle. The exact percentage varies by business — what matters is identifying your highest-value items, not hitting a specific percentage.
How often should I redo ABC analysis?▾
Run quarterly for most businesses. Monthly if you have rapidly changing product mix (fashion, electronics, seasonal). A SKU may move from B to A as it grows, or from A to C as it declines — keeping classifications current ensures management effort stays aligned with actual revenue contribution.
Should I base ABC analysis on revenue or units sold?▾
Use revenue (or ideally gross margin) not units sold. A SKU selling 1,000 units at ₹10 (₹10K revenue) is less important than one selling 50 units at ₹500 (₹25K revenue). Revenue-based classification ensures attention goes to items that matter most financially.
