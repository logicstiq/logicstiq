What is Fill Rate Calculator?

Fill Rate is the percentage of customer demand that you can fulfil immediately from available inventory — without backorders, partial shipments, or substitutions. It is one of the most direct measures of inventory service quality.

Fill rate is different from OTIF: fill rate measures quantity completeness (did you ship 100% of what was ordered?), while OTIF measures both quantity and timing (did you ship 100% on time?). Both are essential service level metrics, but fill rate specifically targets your inventory availability performance.

The Fill Rate Formula

📐 Formula
Fill Rate = (Units Shipped from Stock ÷ Total Units Ordered) × 100

Fill rate measures how well your inventory satisfies customer demand without backorders or lost sales. A fill rate of 95% means 95 out of every 100 units ordered were available and shipped immediately. The remaining 5% either became backorders, partial shipments, or lost sales.

Worked Example

A Flipkart seller received orders for 2,000 units across their catalogue in October. They successfully shipped 1,860 units from available stock. The remaining 140 units were either backorders or lost sales.

📊 Example Calculation
Total units ordered2,000 units
Units shipped from stock1,860 units
Units not fulfilled140 units
Fill Rate(1,860 ÷ 2,000) × 100 = 93%

At 93% fill rate, this seller is missing 7% of potential revenue from unfulfilled demand. For a seller doing ₹50L/month, that 7% gap represents ₹3.5L in lost or delayed revenue — enough to justify better safety stock planning.

Why Fill Rate Matters for Your Business

Fill rate directly translates to revenue. Every percentage point below 100% represents demand you had but could not fulfil — whether that becomes a backorder, a substitution, or a lost sale.

  • Retail compliance: Large retailers like D-Mart and Reliance Retail require minimum fill rates (typically 92–95%) from suppliers
  • Amazon and Flipkart penalties: Chronic low fill rate leads to reduced Buy Box eligibility and suppressed listings
  • Customer retention: Customers who receive partial shipments frequently switch suppliers
  • Safety stock calibration: Fill rate analysis by SKU reveals exactly which items need higher safety stock

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good fill rate for an e-commerce business?▾
For D2C and marketplace sellers in India, a fill rate above 95% is considered good. Top performers target 98%+. Below 90% is a serious problem that will trigger penalties from Amazon, Flipkart, and modern trade retailers. The target varies by category — FMCG targets 97%+ while fashion can be 90-95% due to size and colour variants.
What is the difference between line fill rate and order fill rate?▾
Line fill rate measures the percentage of order lines (individual SKUs) fulfilled completely. Order fill rate measures the percentage of complete orders shipped with every line item in full. Order fill rate is always lower because a single missing SKU fails the entire order. Most retail compliance agreements specify order fill rate as the KPI.
How do I improve my fill rate quickly?▾
The fastest improvements come from: (1) Running ABC analysis to identify your highest-volume SKUs and increasing their safety stock, (2) Setting up reorder point alerts in your ERP so you never accidentally drop below minimum stock, (3) Improving demand forecasting accuracy to anticipate demand spikes before they happen, (4) Adding a secondary supplier for your top 10 highest-volume SKUs.
