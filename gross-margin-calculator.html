📐 Gross Margin Formula
Gross Profit = Revenue − COGS
Gross Margin % = ( Gross Profit ÷ Revenue ) × 100
Markup % = ( Gross Profit ÷ COGS ) × 100
Net Margin % = ( Net Profit After Tax ÷ Revenue ) × 100
What is Gross Margin?

Gross Margin is the percentage of revenue retained after deducting the direct cost of goods sold. It measures how efficiently a business produces or sources its products. A 40% gross margin means for every ₹100 of revenue, ₹40 remains to cover operating expenses and generate profit.

Gross margin is different from markup. Markup is gross profit as a percentage of cost. Margin is gross profit as a percentage of revenue. A 50% markup gives you a 33% gross margin — not 50%. Many Indian businesses confuse the two and misprice products as a result.

📊 Worked Example

A D2C skincare brand selling a serum at ₹999:

Selling price (revenue)₹999
COGS (formulation + packaging + inbound freight)₹380
Gross profit₹619
Gross margin %₹619 ÷ ₹999 = 61.96%
Markup %₹619 ÷ ₹380 = 162.9%
Operating expenses (marketing, fulfilment, overheads)₹280
Net profit before tax₹339 → Net margin 33.9%
Why This Calculator Matters

Pricing Strategy

Gross margin tells you how much room you have for discounts, marketing spend, and operating costs. Selling at a 25% gross margin leaves almost nothing after platform fees and logistics.

Margin vs. Markup Clarity

A 100% markup is only a 50% gross margin. Businesses that target markup instead of margin systematically underprice. This calculator shows both so you always know your true position.

Category Benchmarking

Fashion: 45–65% gross margin. FMCG: 25–40%. Electronics: 10–20%. D2C beauty: 55–70%. If your margin is below category benchmark, your sourcing cost or pricing needs to change.

Investor Readiness

Gross margin is the first metric any investor or banker looks at. A consistently improving gross margin signals operational efficiency and pricing power — both critical for funding conversations.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the difference between gross margin and net margin?
Gross margin = (Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue. It measures product-level profitability. Net margin = (Revenue − COGS − Operating Expenses − Tax) ÷ Revenue. It measures overall business profitability. A business can have a 60% gross margin but a 2% net margin if operating expenses are very high — common in D2C brands with heavy marketing spend.
What gross margin do I need to be profitable on Amazon India?
Amazon India charges 5–15% referral fee + 2–4% closing fee + GST on fees. Add FBA fulfilment (₹30–80 per unit depending on size) and FBA storage. A product selling at ₹500 needs at least 50% gross margin (₹250 COGS) to have any chance of net profitability after all Amazon fees and operating costs.
How do I improve my gross margin?
Three levers: (1) Reduce COGS — negotiate better prices with suppliers, increase order quantities to get volume discounts, reduce inbound freight cost, (2) Increase selling price — test price elasticity, add value through branding or bundling, (3) Change product mix — discontinue low-margin SKUs, promote high-margin products. Improving gross margin by 5% is often worth more than 20% more revenue.
Should I include GST in gross margin calculation?
If you are a GST-registered business and recover GST as ITC, exclude GST from both revenue and COGS (use net-of-GST figures). This gives a true gross margin. If you are not GST-registered or have non-recoverable GST, include it in COGS as a real cost.
