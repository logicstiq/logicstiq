⚡ Worked Example · Quick Commerce

How an FMCG brand plans Blinkit & Zepto dark-store replenishment

Quick commerce breaks the usual rules: demand is per dark store, lead times are 1–2 days, and stockouts are punished instantly. Here's how to plan replenishment that keeps a hero SKU live across every store.

ℹ️ Illustrative worked example — representative brand and numbers, not a real named customer. Validate against your own store-level data before ordering.

The situation

A snacks brand supplies a hero SKU to Blinkit and Zepto across 40 dark stores in Bengaluru. Quick-commerce buyers expect a 10-minute delivery, so a single out-of-stock store loses the sale and hurts visibility in the app. Because lead times are short but demand is spiky by store and time-of-day, planning at the brand level is useless — it has to be per store.

Step 1 — Work in daily velocity, per store

Forget monthly averages. A busy store sells ~45 units/day of the hero SKU; a quieter one ~18. Plan each store on its own daily run-rate.

Busy store: 45 units/day Quiet store: 18 units/day

Upload store-level sales to the AI Demand Planner and set Commerce Type to Quick Commerce — it plans by city and dark store with daily velocity.

⚡ Open the AI Demand Planner
Step 2 — Higher service level, short lead time

Q-comm targets ~98% service (Z = 2.05) because stockouts are so costly, but the lead time is short (2 days), which keeps the buffer small. For the busy store (σd 12 units):

Safety stock = Z × σd × √LT = 2.05 × 12 × √2 ≈ 2.05 × 12 × 1.41 ≈ 35 units
🛡️ Safety Stock Calculator
Step 3 — Per-store reorder point

Trigger a top-up when a store's on-hand hits:

Reorder point = (daily velocity × lead time) + safety stock Busy store = (45 × 2) + 35 = 125 units Quiet store = (18 × 2) + 15 = 51 units
🎯 Reorder Point Calculator
Step 4 — Roll up to a city dispatch plan

Sum each store's gap-to-target into one city order, then split the delivery by store. High-velocity stores get topped up more frequently; weekend and evening peaks get a small extra buffer.

Putting the numbers together

Busy store45 units/day35 units125 units
Quiet store18 units/day15 units51 units

✅ The outcome

Each store gets its own reorder point and top-up rhythm instead of a one-size-fits-all number. Busy stores stay in stock through evening peaks, quiet stores don't get overloaded, and the brand holds availability (and app visibility) across all 40 stores — the metric quick-commerce platforms reward.
