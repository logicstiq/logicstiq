📐 Break-Even Formula
Contribution Margin = Selling Price − Variable Cost per Unit
Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution Margin
Break-Even Revenue = Break-Even Units × Selling Price
Margin of Safety = Current Sales − Break-Even Sales
What is the Break-Even Calculator?

The break-even point is the level of sales at which total revenue exactly equals total costs — you are making neither profit nor loss. Below break-even, you lose money. Above it, every unit sold generates profit equal to your contribution margin per unit.

For Indian SMEs, D2C brands, and marketplace sellers, break-even analysis is the most fundamental financial check before launching a product, setting a price, or committing to a warehouse lease. It answers: "How much do I need to sell just to cover my costs?"

📊 Worked Example

A D2C skincare brand selling on Nykaa and their own website:

Fixed costs per month (rent, salaries, ads)₹1,50,000
Selling price per unit₹599
Variable cost per unit (COGS + packaging + shipping)₹280
Contribution margin per unit₹319
Break-Even Units₹1,50,000 ÷ ₹319 = 470 units/month
Break-Even Revenue₹2,81,530/month
Why Break-Even Analysis Matters

Pricing Decisions

Understand how a price increase or discount changes your break-even point. A 10% discount on ₹599 raises your break-even from 470 units to 560 units — a 19% jump in required volume.

New Product Launch

Before launching any new SKU, calculate break-even. If the required volume is higher than your realistic demand forecast, the product economics don't work at that price point.

Fixed Cost Decisions

Adding a new warehouse, hiring a team member, or subscribing to a software tool changes your fixed costs and raises your break-even. Quantify this before committing.

Investor Conversations

VCs and angel investors always ask about break-even. A clear, data-backed break-even analysis demonstrates financial literacy and business model viability.

Frequently Asked Questions
What counts as a fixed cost vs. variable cost?
Fixed costs stay constant regardless of how many units you sell: warehouse rent, staff salaries, software subscriptions, loan EMIs, annual licences. Variable costs change with every unit sold: product cost (COGS), packaging, outbound shipping, marketplace commission (Amazon/Flipkart %). Some costs are semi-variable — electricity, for example — use your best estimate of the portion that is truly fixed.
Should I include Amazon/Flipkart commission in variable cost?
Yes. Marketplace commissions (typically 5–15% of selling price depending on category) are per-unit costs — the more you sell, the more commission you pay. Include them in your variable cost per unit alongside COGS and packaging. This gives you a true contribution margin that reflects the real economics of your marketplace channel.
What is Margin of Safety?
Margin of Safety is how far your current sales are above the break-even point — expressed in units or revenue. If your break-even is 470 units/month and you are selling 800 units, your margin of safety is 330 units (41%). A higher margin of safety means more cushion if sales drop. A margin of safety below 20% signals a vulnerable business model.
How does break-even change if I give a discount?
Discounts reduce your selling price while variable costs stay the same — so contribution margin per unit falls and break-even rises. For example, a 10% discount on a ₹500 product with ₹300 variable cost reduces contribution margin from ₹200 to ₹150, raising break-even by 33%. Always run the break-even calculation before approving discount campaigns.
