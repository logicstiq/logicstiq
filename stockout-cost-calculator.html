What is Stockout Cost Calculator?

A stockout occurs when customer demand exists but no inventory is available to fulfil it. Stockouts cost more than just one lost sale — they affect customer relationships, marketplace rankings, OTIF scores, and sometimes trigger formal financial penalties from large retailers.

On Amazon and Flipkart, going out of stock causes your listing rank to drop, sometimes permanently. The marketplace algorithms penalise sellers who stockout by reducing their visibility even after inventory is restored. This invisible, downstream cost is often larger than the direct lost sales during the stockout period.

The Stockout Cost Formula

📐 Formula
Stockout Cost = (Days OOS × Daily Units × Margin per Unit) + Expediting Cost + Penalties

Stockout cost has three components: (1) Direct lost margin on unfulfilled demand, (2) Expediting costs to source product urgently from alternative suppliers at premium prices, (3) Customer penalties such as OTIF chargebacks from retailers.

Worked Example

A Blinkit supplier runs out of stock on a fast-moving product for 4 days. Their daily sales are 120 units at ₹200/unit with 40% margin. They also receive a ₹8,000 chargeback from Blinkit.

📊 Example Calculation
Days out of stock4 days
Daily sales lost120 units × ₹200 = ₹24,000/day
Revenue lost4 × ₹24,000 = ₹96,000
Margin lost (40%)₹96,000 × 40% = ₹38,400
Blinkit chargeback₹8,000
Total Stockout Cost₹38,400 + ₹8,000 = ₹46,400

₹46,400 for a 4-day stockout. The safety stock needed to prevent this — approximately 480 units (4 days × 120 units) — costs about ₹16,000 to hold for a month at 25% annual carrying cost. The stockout was 3x more expensive than the safety stock would have cost.

Why Stockout Cost Matters for Your Business

The key insight from stockout cost calculation is that safety stock is almost always cheaper than the stockout it prevents. Most businesses hold too little safety stock because they see only the holding cost — not the stockout cost they are avoiding.

  • Safety stock justification: Compare safety stock carrying cost vs. stockout probability × stockout cost
  • SKU prioritisation: Calculate stockout cost per SKU to decide which ones deserve highest safety stock
  • Supplier SLA: Use stockout cost data to negotiate penalty clauses into supplier contracts
  • Amazon/Flipkart: Rank drop cost (invisible, long-term) often exceeds direct lost margin

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate the cost of a stockout on Amazon?▾
For Amazon sellers, stockout cost = Direct lost margin + Rank recovery cost. Amazon's A9 algorithm drops your keyword ranking when you stockout, meaning even after restocking, you sell less than before. Industry estimates suggest Amazon rank recovery takes 2-4 weeks of above-baseline sales, effectively extending the financial impact of a 1-week stockout to 3-5 weeks.
Is expediting always necessary during a stockout?▾
Not always. For B2B stockouts, expediting (sourcing from a secondary supplier at premium price) is often necessary to maintain customer relationships. For B2C/marketplace stockouts, you cannot substitute — you either have the product or you don't. Always factor expediting cost for your key accounts where a stockout could trigger formal penalties or order cancellation.
How much safety stock should I hold to prevent stockouts?▾
Safety stock level depends on your service level target and demand variability. The basic formula is: Safety Stock = Z × σ_lead × √Lead Time, where Z is your service level factor (1.65 for 95%, 2.05 for 98%), and σ_lead is standard deviation of lead time demand.
