What does OTIF mean?

OTIF stands for On-Time In-Full. It is the single most important delivery performance metric in supply chain management. An order is OTIF compliant only if it meets two conditions simultaneously:

  • On-Time: The order arrives on or before the agreed delivery date
  • In-Full: The order arrives with 100% of the requested quantity — no short shipments

If an order arrives on time but is missing 2 units — it fails OTIF. If the order is complete but arrives one day late — it fails OTIF. Both conditions must be met for every single order for it to count as OTIF compliant.

💡
Why OTIF matters: OTIF measures your reliability as a supplier or logistics operation. A consistently high OTIF score builds trust with buyers, retailers, and marketplaces. A low OTIF score costs you money in penalties, chargebacks, and lost repeat orders — sometimes before you even know there's a problem.

OTIF vs individual On-Time and In-Full metrics

Many operations track On-Time delivery and In-Full fill rate separately. This hides a critical problem — an operation could be 95% on-time and 95% in-full, yet their combined OTIF is only 90.25% (0.95 × 0.95). OTIF's strength is that it measures both constraints together, giving a true picture of supply chain reliability.

The OTIF formula

📐 OTIF Formula
OTIF % = (Orders delivered On-Time AND In-Full ÷ Total Orders) × 100
An order counts as OTIF only if it satisfies BOTH conditions.
One failure on either dimension = the entire order fails OTIF.

Breaking it down further

📐 On-Time Rate
OT % = (Orders delivered on or before due date ÷ Total Orders) × 100
📐 In-Full Rate
IF % = (Orders with 100% quantity delivered ÷ Total Orders) × 100
📐 Combined OTIF (approximation)
OTIF ≈ OT% × IF% ÷ 100
Note: This approximation works when OT and IF failures are independent. For exact OTIF, count orders meeting BOTH criteria simultaneously.

Walmart & Amazon OTIF fines — what sellers need to know

OTIF is not just a performance metric — for suppliers to large retailers, it has direct financial consequences:

Walmart OTIF requirement

Walmart requires a minimum 98% OTIF for suppliers shipping to their US distribution centres. Suppliers who fall below this threshold face a financial penalty of 3% of the cost of goods for the non-compliant shipments. Walmart introduced this policy in 2017 and has tightened it progressively since. For a supplier shipping ₹1 crore of goods per month to Walmart, a 95% OTIF instead of 98% triggers a ₹9L penalty.

Amazon Vendor Central

Amazon tracks OTIF for vendors (first-party sellers on Amazon). Vendors below 90% OTIF face chargebacks, reduced purchase order volumes, and in extreme cases, removal from the vendor programme. Amazon also tracks Receive Fill Rate (RFR) — the in-full component — separately and applies chargebacks when RFR falls below 95%.

Flipkart & Indian modern trade

Major Indian retailers including Reliance Retail, D-Mart, and Big Bazaar have introduced OTIF-based penalty clauses in supplier agreements. Typically: OTIF below 90% triggers a 1–2% deduction from the invoice value. Some retailers are moving to daily OTIF tracking, particularly for fresh and fast-moving categories.

🚨
The compounding penalty problem: OTIF fines are calculated on the value of non-compliant orders, not your total shipment value. If you ship ₹50L in a month at 85% OTIF and your retailer charges 3% on non-compliant orders (15% of ₹50L = ₹7.5L at risk), your penalty is ₹22,500. But the reputational cost — reduced shelf space, lower PO volumes, delisting risk — is far higher than the financial penalty.

7 proven ways to improve your OTIF score

01
Fix demand forecasting first
Most OTIF failures start with bad demand forecasts. If you don't know what's coming, you can't have the stock to ship. Accurate 30–90 day forecasts prevent the "we don't have it" In-Full failures that crush OTIF scores.
02
Set realistic lead times
On-Time failures often come from over-promising on delivery dates. Add buffer to your committed delivery windows — underpromise and overdeliver. A conservative commitment met consistently beats an optimistic one missed frequently.
03
Track OTIF by SKU, not just overall
An 88% overall OTIF often hides specific SKUs with 60% OTIF dragging everything down. Break OTIF analysis to SKU level to find the 5–10 products causing most failures.
04
Build carrier performance scorecards
Track On-Time delivery by carrier. If Blue Dart is 97% on-time and DTDC is 82%, route time-sensitive orders through the better carrier. Carrier selection is one of the fastest OTIF improvement levers.
05
Increase safety stock on high-OTIF accounts
For your key accounts where OTIF penalties apply, maintain higher safety stock buffers specifically for their orders. The cost of holding extra inventory is almost always lower than the OTIF fine.
06
Fix your order confirmation process
In-Full failures often happen because orders are accepted without available-to-promise checking. Before confirming an order, verify current stock + inbound minus committed orders. Never confirm what you cannot ship in full.
07
Weekly OTIF review meetings
OTIF improves when it is visible and discussed regularly. A weekly 15-minute review of last week's OTIF, root causes of failures, and corrective actions is the single most impactful operational habit for sustained OTIF improvement.
