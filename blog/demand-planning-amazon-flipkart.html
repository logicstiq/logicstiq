Why demand planning matters more than ever in 2025

If you're selling on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, or Myntra in 2025, your competition has changed. Three years ago, having a good product was enough. Today, the sellers winning on marketplace rankings aren't just the ones with the best products — they're the ones who never run out of stock.

Amazon's A9 algorithm actively penalises stockouts. When your listing goes out of stock, you lose your rank, your sponsored ads stop delivering, your BSR drops, and recovery takes weeks — not hours. Every day out of stock on a fast-moving SKU can cost ₹10,000 to ₹10 lakh depending on your sales velocity.

🚨
The marketplace seller's nightmare: You have your best ever Diwali. Sales are 6x normal. You run out of stock on Day 5 of the sale. Your listing disappears from search. Competitors take your spot. You spend 3 months recovering your rank. This is 100% preventable — with demand planning.

On Flipkart, going out of stock during Big Billion Days removes you from promotional slots you may have paid for. On Meesho, consistent availability is a key factor in the seller scoring algorithm that determines your organic visibility.

The 3-step demand planning framework

1
Build your baseline demand forecast
Look at your last 3–6 months of sales data per SKU. Calculate your average daily sales velocity — units sold per day. This is your baseline: what you'd expect to sell in a normal month with no events, no promotions, no seasonal adjustment applied yet.
2
Apply seasonality and event multipliers
Layer in known demand spikes from the seasonality calendar below. If you sell electronics, Diwali demand is typically 3–5x normal. If you sell festive clothing, Navratri and Dussehra can be 4–6x. Use last year's actual sales if you have them — or use the industry benchmarks below.
3
Calculate how much to stock & when to reorder
Take your forecast demand for the next 60–90 days, add safety stock (buffer for demand spikes and supply delays), subtract your current inventory, and place your purchase order for the difference. Factor in your supplier's lead time so stock arrives before you need it.
📐 How Much to Order
Order Qty = (Forecast Demand × Cover Period) + Safety Stock − Current Stock − Inbound
📌
Example: Your earbuds sell 50 units/day. You want 60 days of cover. Safety stock = 7 days (350 units). Current stock = 800. Inbound = 200.

Order Qty = (50 × 60) + 350 − 800 − 200 = 2,350 units

Platform-specific demand planning tips

🛒
Amazon India
Use the "Restock Inventory" report in Seller Central for Amazon's demand forecast. Cross-check with your own historical data — Amazon is often conservative. Add 30–40% buffer during Prime Day and the festive season.
🛍️
Flipkart
Flipkart's "Inventory Health" dashboard shows demand signals. Big Billion Days can drive 8x normal demand for top sellers. Watch for seller communications 12 weeks before BBD — act immediately when they arrive.
🧡
Meesho
Meesho demand is driven heavily by reseller activity — spikes are harder to predict. Keep 45 days of safety stock for top SKUs. Meesho's festive events drive 3–4x normal demand across most categories.
👗
Myntra
End of Season Sales (EORS) in June and December are Myntra's biggest events. Fashion demand is highly trend-driven — use 30-day rolling averages rather than longer windows to capture trend changes faster.
💄
Nykaa
Strong gifting spikes around Valentine's Day, Diwali, and Women's Day. Nykaa Pink Friday (November) rivals Big Billion Days for the beauty category. Plan 10 weeks in advance for peak events.
🌙
Noon (Middle East)
Ramadan and Eid al-Fitr are the biggest demand spikes — 2–3x for most categories, 4–5x for gifting and fashion. White Friday (November) is the region's biggest sale event of the year.

What a stockout actually costs you

Most sellers underestimate the true cost of going out of stock. It's not just the lost sales during the stockout period — it's the compounding effect on your marketplace metrics:

  • Lost immediate sales — you can't sell what you don't have
  • Rank drop — Amazon's algorithm demotes listings that go out of stock. Recovery takes 2–8 weeks of consistent sales velocity
  • Ad spend wasted — if Sponsored Ads are running and you go out of stock, every click that converts results in zero because there's nothing to buy
  • Competitor gains — your stockout is your competitor's opportunity. They gain rank and reviews during your absence
  • Sale event exclusion — platforms exclude out-of-stock listings from promotional events, meaning you miss your biggest revenue days of the year
📉
Real cost example: An earbuds seller averaging ₹2L/day goes out of stock for 5 days during Diwali. Direct lost revenue: ₹10L. But rank drops 40 positions. Recovery takes 3 weeks. Total estimated cost including the recovery period: ₹25–40L. This is the real cost of missing demand planning.

Quick commerce demand — Blinkit, Zepto, Swiggy Instamart

Q-commerce has unique demand characteristics that traditional planning doesn't account for:

  • Hyperlocal demand — varies massively by city, area, and time of day. A product selling 200 units/day in Bangalore Koramangala may sell 10/day in Pune
  • Hour-of-day peaks — demand spikes at 7–9 AM (morning rush), 12–2 PM (lunch), and 8–10 PM (evening peak)
  • Dark store replenishment — you're replenishing individual dark stores, not a central warehouse. Each needs its own forecast
  • Zero stockout tolerance — being out of stock for even 2 hours during peak time means losing those orders permanently
⚡
Q-Commerce planning rule: Maintain a minimum of 3 days of stock at each dark store at all times. Set your replenishment trigger at 1.5 days of stock remaining. This gives you buffer for demand spikes without over-investing in hyperlocal inventory.
