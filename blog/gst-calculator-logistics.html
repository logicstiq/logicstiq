India — Complete Tax Guide 2025

GST rates on freight and logistics services

India's GST system applies different rates to different types of transportation services. Understanding which rate applies to your specific freight transaction is essential for accurate costing, correct ITC claims, and compliance.

Service TypeGST RateSAC CodeNotes
Road transport by GTA (Goods Transport Agency) 5% 9965 GTA pays under Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM). No ITC allowed at 5% rate.
Road transport by GTA (Optional forward charge) 12% 9965 GTA opts for 12% forward charge — ITC allowed for service receiver.
Courier services (by air, road) 18% 9968 Applies to Shiprocket, Delhivery, Blue Dart, DTDC, XpressBees etc.
Air freight — domestic 18% 9965 Including air cargo services.
Air freight — international (export) 0% (Zero-rated) 9965 Export services are zero-rated. ITC refund available.
Rail freight 5% 9965 Indian Railways freight transport.
Sea freight — coastal shipping 5% 9965 Domestic sea transport of goods.
Warehousing services 18% 9967 Storage and warehousing of non-agricultural goods.
Warehousing — agricultural produce Exempt 9967 Specific exemption for agricultural produce storage.
Freight forwarding services 18% 9965 Freight forwarder / customs broker charges.
Packing and packaging services 18% 9988 Contract packing services.
Loading/unloading labour 18% 9985 If supplied through a contractor. Direct labour may be exempt.

Goods Transport Agency (GTA) — special rules

A Goods Transport Agency (GTA) is any person who provides road transport services using a consignment note (bilty). GTAs have a unique position under GST with two rate options and specific Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM) rules:

Option 1 — 5% GST under Reverse Charge Mechanism (Default)

Under RCM, the recipient of the GTA service (the shipper/consignee) is liable to pay GST — not the transporter. The registered business receiving the freight service must self-assess and pay 5% GST directly to the government.

⚠️
Who pays RCM on GTA services? Only registered GST entities are liable under RCM. If you are a registered manufacturer, distributor, or e-commerce company receiving road freight services from a GTA, you must pay 5% GST under RCM. However, ITC cannot be claimed on RCM paid at the 5% rate — this is a real cost to your business.

Option 2 — 12% GST under Forward Charge (GTA's choice)

A GTA can opt to pay GST under forward charge at 12%. In this case the GTA pays the tax, includes it in their invoice, and the service receiver can claim ITC on the 12% paid. This option is generally better for businesses with significant freight costs who want to claim ITC.

Courier and express delivery services

Courier services — including Shiprocket, Delhivery, Blue Dart, XpressBees, Ecom Express, DTDC, and India Post Express — are classified under SAC code 9968 and attract 18% GST under forward charge.

This is the freight cost most D2C brands and marketplace sellers face daily. For an e-commerce business shipping 1,000 orders/day at ₹60/shipment average freight:

  • Monthly freight cost: ₹18,00,000 (30 days × 1,000 × ₹60)
  • GST at 18%: ₹3,24,000/month
  • Annual GST on freight: ₹38,88,000

Whether you can claim ITC on this GST is one of the most important questions for your profitability.

Input Tax Credit (ITC) on freight — who can claim it?

ITC allows you to offset GST paid on inputs (including freight) against your output GST liability. However, ITC on freight is not universally available — the rules are specific:

✅ ITC Allowed
  • Courier services (18%) used for business purposes where you are a registered GST taxpayer
  • GTA services at 12% (forward charge) — ITC on the 12% paid
  • Air freight for domestic B2B supply of goods
  • Warehouse services used for taxable goods
  • Freight for outward supply of taxable goods
❌ ITC Blocked / Not Allowed
  • GTA services at 5% (RCM) — ITC specifically blocked under Section 17(5)
  • Freight for supply of exempted goods
  • Personal transportation (not related to business)
  • Freight for food and beverages for employees
  • Motor vehicle insurance (separate restriction)
🚨
The RCM trap: Many businesses pay 5% RCM on GTA services and assume they can claim the ITC back. They cannot. GST Section 17(5)(g) specifically blocks ITC on motor vehicle services including road freight under RCM at 5%. If your GTA agrees to charge 12% forward charge instead, you can claim full ITC on that amount. Ask your transporters which rate they are registered under.

E-Way Bill — when is it required?

An e-way bill is a mandatory electronic document required for movement of goods valued above ₹50,000 (consignment value including GST) within India. Key rules:

  • Threshold: ₹50,000+ consignment value — e-way bill mandatory. Below ₹50,000 — not required (with exceptions for some notified goods)
  • Who generates it: The supplier, transporter, or recipient can generate the e-way bill on the GST portal (ewaybillgst.gov.in)
  • Validity: 1 day for every 200 km for regular cargo. Multi-modal or over-dimensional cargo have different rules
  • Intra-state: Most states require e-way bills for intra-state movement too — check your state's threshold (some states have lower thresholds like ₹10,000)
  • Exemptions: Agricultural produce, milk, eggs, unprocessed food, currency, personal luggage, and some others are exempt from e-way bill requirements
  • Penalty for non-compliance: ₹10,000 or tax evaded (whichever is higher) plus potential seizure of goods and vehicle
✅
E-Way Bill for e-commerce: If you are fulfilling orders through Amazon FBA, Flipkart Fulfilment, or your own courier, the courier company typically generates the e-way bill on your behalf. However, you remain responsible for ensuring it is generated correctly. Always verify e-way bill numbers on high-value consignments.
