What is Warehouse Space Calculator?

Warehouse space planning ensures you have adequate storage for your inventory without paying for excess space. Getting this wrong in either direction is costly — too little space causes operational chaos, too much space means wasted rent.

For D2C brands and distributors using third-party logistics (3PL) providers, accurate space calculation helps negotiate storage contracts, plan seasonal peak capacity, and budget monthly 3PL storage costs accurately.

The Warehouse Space Formula

📐 Formula
Warehouse Space = Pallets × Pallet Footprint × Aisle Space Factor

The aisle space factor accounts for the space needed for forklift or trolley movement between storage bays. A factor of 1.5 means for every 1 sq ft of actual storage, you need 0.5 sq ft of access aisle. Typical factors: hand truck warehouse 1.5, forklift warehouse 2.0-2.5, high-rack narrow aisle 1.3.

Worked Example

A FMCG distributor needs to store 300 pallets. Each pallet occupies 12 sq ft. They use forklifts requiring an aisle factor of 2.0.

The distributor needs 7,200 sq ft — even though only 3,600 sq ft is directly occupied by pallets. The remaining 3,600 sq ft is essential access space for safe forklift operation.

Why Warehouse Space Matters for Your Business

Accurate space planning prevents the two most common warehouse planning mistakes: under-planning that creates operational chaos during peak season, and over-planning that wastes rent money year-round.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good warehouse utilisation rate?▾
Industry best practice is 80-85% storage utilisation. Above 90% makes operations difficult — pickers can't maneuver efficiently. Below 70% means you are paying for unused space. Most warehouse managers target 80% utilisation during normal operations with planned capacity for peak season spikes.
How do I calculate warehouse space needed for Blinkit or Zepto dark stores?▾
Dark stores require much higher storage density than traditional warehouses because products are small and picked individually. Dark stores typically use racking systems and achieve 250-400 SKUs per 1,000 sq ft. Calculate based on SKU count and average unit dimensions rather than pallet equivalents for dark store planning.
What is the average 3PL storage cost per pallet per month in India?▾
3PL storage costs in India typically range from ₹300-600 per pallet per month for ambient storage in major cities. Cold storage costs ₹800-1,500 per pallet per month. Rates vary by location (Mumbai/Delhi premium vs. Tier 2 cities), contract volume, and services included.
