📐 Shipping Cost Per Unit Formula
Courier Per Unit = Shipment Freight ÷ Units Per Order
Pack & Handle = (Packaging + Labour) ÷ Units Per Order
Returns Provision = Return Rate% × Return Cost ÷ 100
Total Per Unit = Courier + Pack & Handle + Returns Provision
What is Shipping Cost Per Unit?

Shipping cost per unit is the true all-in cost of getting one unit from your warehouse to your customer's door — and accounting for the fraction that comes back as a return. Most businesses only count the courier charge and miss packaging, handling labour, and returns, understating fulfilment cost by 30–50%.

For D2C brands and marketplace sellers in India, fulfilment cost is typically the second or third largest cost after COGS and marketing. Knowing it per unit lets you compare channels (own website vs. Amazon vs. Flipkart), evaluate 3PL vs. in-house fulfilment, and set minimum selling prices correctly.

Why This Calculator Matters

True Margin Visibility

A product selling at ₹599 with ₹240 COGS looks like 60% gross margin. Add ₹123 fulfilment + ₹90 marketing CAC and you're left with ₹146 (24%) — a very different picture.

3PL vs. In-House Decision

Calculate your in-house fulfilment cost per unit here, then compare with 3PL quotes. If 3PL is within 15–20% of your in-house cost, outsourcing is often better — freeing management bandwidth and capex.

Channel Comparison

Your own website may have lower platform fees than Amazon, but if your fulfilment cost is higher (no FBA economies of scale), the net margin per unit might actually be lower. Always compare on total fulfilment cost.

Return Rate Impact

Fashion returns in India run 20–35%. A 20% return rate at ₹150 return cost adds ₹30 per unit shipped to your fulfilment cost — equivalent to a 4–5% margin hit on a ₹700 product.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the average shipping cost per unit for D2C brands in India?
For B2C courier (single units, 500g–1kg, within zone): ₹50–90 via Delhivery/Shiprocket. Add packaging (₹10–20), handling (₹8–15), and returns provision (₹15–40 depending on return rate and category). Total per-unit fulfilment cost typically ranges ₹80–150 for fashion and ₹60–100 for FMCG.
Should I include Amazon FBA fees in this calculator?
For Amazon FBA, replace 'Courier Charge' with your FBA fulfilment fee per unit (check your Amazon Seller Central fee preview — typically ₹30–80 for standard size). Keep packaging and handling as 0 since Amazon handles that. Add the FBA return processing fee in return cost.
How do I reduce per-unit shipping cost?
Five levers: (1) Negotiate volume-based rates with 2–3 carriers, (2) Use a multi-carrier aggregator like Shiprocket that auto-selects cheapest carrier, (3) Right-size packaging to reduce volumetric weight charges, (4) Reduce return rate through better product descriptions, size guides, and quality control, (5) Consolidate multi-SKU orders into one shipment where possible.
How does return rate affect per-unit cost?
Every percentage point of return rate adds (Return Handling Cost ÷ 100) to your per-unit fulfilment cost. At ₹150 return handling cost, each 1% of return rate adds ₹1.50 per unit. Reducing return rate from 20% to 15% saves ₹7.50 per unit — equivalent to ₹75,000 per month at 10,000 units/month scale.
