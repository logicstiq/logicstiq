What is Supply Chain Disruption Impact Calculator?

A supply chain disruption is any event that interrupts the normal flow of goods through your supply chain — a supplier factory shutdown, a port congestion event, a truckers' strike, a delayed shipment, or a natural disaster affecting logistics routes.

Quantifying disruption cost enables two important decisions: (1) Justifying investment in resilience measures like safety stock and dual sourcing — compare the cost of the resilience investment vs. the expected disruption cost, and (2) Prioritising which supply chain risks to mitigate first based on their financial impact.

The Disruption Impact Formula

📐 Formula
Disruption Cost = (Duration × Daily Margin Lost) + Expediting + Penalties + (Recovery Days × Daily Margin × 0.5)

The recovery period accounts for the fact that even after a disruption ends, operations do not immediately return to full capacity — there is typically a ramp-up period where some revenue and margin is still being lost at a reduced rate.

Worked Example

A FMCG brand experiences a 5-day port congestion delay at Nhava Sheva affecting their primary import shipment. Daily revenue impact: ₹80,000.

Daily margin lost₹80,000 × 35% = ₹28,000/day
Margin lost during disruption5 × ₹28,000 = ₹1,40,000
Air freight expediting cost₹45,000
Recovery period (3 days at 50%)₹42,000
Total Disruption Cost₹2,27,000

₹2.27 lakh for a 5-day port delay. Safety stock to cover 5 days at this revenue level costs approximately ₹20,000-25,000 per month to hold. The disruption cost is 9-10 months of safety stock carrying cost — making the safety stock investment clearly justified.

Why Disruption Impact Matters for Your Business

Most supply chain decisions are made without quantifying the cost of disruption risk. This leads to systematic underinvestment in resilience — until a major disruption makes the cost painfully visible.

  • Safety stock ROI: Compare safety stock cost vs. disruption probability × disruption cost
  • Dual sourcing justification: Second supplier premium vs. expected disruption cost from single sourcing
  • Insurance decisions: Cargo and business interruption insurance cost vs. disruption financial exposure
  • Board reporting: Quantify supply chain risk in financial terms for leadership and investor reporting

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common supply chain disruptions in India?▾
Common disruptions in India: Port congestion at Nhava Sheva, Mundra, and Chennai (especially pre-Diwali season), truckers' strikes and fuel price-related route disruptions, monsoon-related delays in road transport (June-September), supplier capacity constraints during festive season when all manufacturers are at full capacity, and custom clearance delays for imported goods.
How do I calculate supply chain risk exposure?▾
Risk exposure = Probability of disruption × Impact of disruption. For each critical supply chain risk, estimate: (1) How likely is this disruption in the next 12 months (%), and (2) What is the financial impact if it occurs? Multiply them for risk-adjusted expected loss. Prioritise the risks with the highest expected loss for mitigation investment.
What is business continuity planning for supply chains?▾
Supply chain business continuity planning (BCP) identifies critical supply chain nodes and develops response plans for major disruption scenarios. Key elements: Identify single points of failure (single-source suppliers, single logistics routes), Develop alternative sources or routes for each critical node, Maintain minimum strategic inventory of critical items, Establish emergency communication protocols with key suppliers and customers.
