📐 Volumetric Weight Formula
Volumetric Weight (kg) = ( L × W × H in cm ) ÷ Divisor
Chargeable Weight = MAX ( Actual Weight, Volumetric Weight )
Divisors: Express courier ÷5000 · Surface ÷6000 · DTDC B2C ÷4000
What is Volumetric Weight?

Volumetric weight (also called dimensional weight or DIM weight) is a pricing method used by couriers that accounts for the space a package takes up relative to its actual weight. When a package is large but light, the courier charges based on volumetric weight because the package occupies space that could carry heavier, more revenue-generating freight.

Indian couriers — Delhivery, Shiprocket, Blue Dart, DTDC, XpressBees — all apply volumetric weight. If volumetric weight exceeds actual weight, you are charged for the volumetric weight. Missing this calculation leads to systematic underestimation of freight cost and margin erosion.

Frequently Asked Questions
Should I measure the product or the shipping box?
Always measure the outer shipping carton — not the product inside. Couriers scan the outer box using dimensional scanners. Include all packaging materials (bubble wrap, foam inserts) in your measurement.
What is the GST rate on courier freight in India?
Courier services (Delhivery, Shiprocket, Blue Dart, XpressBees, DTDC) attract 18% GST under SAC code 9968. If you are a GST-registered business, you can claim full ITC on this 18% GST against your output liability.
Why do different couriers use different divisors?
The divisor is a commercial decision by each carrier reflecting how they value cubic space vs. weight capacity. Lower divisors (4000) penalise bulky shipments more. Always check your specific contract rate card — divisors can vary even within the same carrier based on your contract tier and volume.
How do I reduce volumetric weight charges?
Use right-sized packaging to eliminate void space. Switch to variable-size boxes. Use vacuum compression for soft goods like clothing and bedding. Even reducing one dimension by 3–5 cm can drop you to a lower chargeable weight slab and save meaningful cost at scale.
