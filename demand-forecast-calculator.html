What is Demand Forecast Calculator?

Demand forecasting is the process of estimating future customer demand based on historical sales data and relevant factors. Even a simple statistical forecast is far more accurate than gut feel — and prevents both overstock and stockout situations.

For most Indian SMEs, a simple 3-period weighted moving average is sufficient for monthly demand planning. More sophisticated methods (exponential smoothing, ARIMA, machine learning) offer marginal improvement for the additional complexity — and LogicstIQ's AI Demand Planner handles those automatically when you upload your full sales file.

The Demand Forecast Formula

📐 Formula
3-Period Moving Average = (P4 + P5 + P6) ÷ 3 | Weighted: (P4×0.2 + P5×0.3 + P6×0.5)

Moving average treats all past periods equally. Weighted average gives more importance to recent periods (weights: oldest 20%, middle 30%, most recent 50%). Weighted average responds faster to demand changes but is more sensitive to recent anomalies. Use weighted for trending products, simple moving average for stable demand.

Worked Example

A Meesho seller forecasts next month's demand for a kurti using the last 6 months of sales data:

3-Month Moving Avg(410+490+470) ÷ 3 = 457 units
Weighted Forecast(410×0.2)+(490×0.3)+(470×0.5) = 464 units

The upward trend (320→470) means the weighted forecast (464) is more reliable here than simple moving average (457) because it gives more weight to recent higher months. This seller should plan for approximately 460-470 units next month.

Why Demand Forecast Matters for Your Business

Better demand forecasts reduce both overstock (dead inventory) and stockouts (lost sales). A 10% improvement in forecast accuracy can reduce required inventory by 15-20%.

  • Purchase order planning: Order the right quantity based on expected demand, not historical averages
  • Safety stock efficiency: Accurate forecasts reduce the safety stock buffer needed for the same service level
  • Seasonal planning: Build seasonal uplift into forecasts using the India seasonality calendar
  • Cash flow: Avoid over-ordering by right-sizing purchase quantities to forecasted demand

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between demand forecasting and demand planning?▾
Demand forecasting is the statistical process of predicting future demand using historical data. Demand planning is broader — it takes the forecast and translates it into operational decisions: what to order, when to order, how much safety stock to hold, and how to handle demand variability. LogicstIQ's AI Demand Planner does both: it generates forecasts and translates them into a complete reorder plan.
How accurate is a simple moving average forecast?▾
For stable, non-seasonal products, a 3-period moving average typically achieves Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) of 15-25%. For seasonal products or products with strong trends, accuracy degrades significantly. Adding seasonality factors and using weighted averages improves accuracy to 10-15% MAPE for many products. LogicstIQ's AI Demand Planner incorporates Indian seasonality (Diwali, Big Billion Days, etc.) automatically.
How do I handle Diwali and seasonal demand spikes in my forecast?▾
Simple moving averages will underforecast during Diwali and overforecast after. The correct approach: (1) Identify your seasonal lift factor — e.g., Diwali month typically sells 2.3x normal, (2) Multiply your base forecast by the seasonal factor for the festive period, (3) Plan purchase orders 8-12 weeks in advance given Indian supply chain lead times.
