📦 EOQ Calculator

Calculate your Economic Order Quantity instantly — the optimal order size that minimises total inventory costs.

📐 The Formula
EOQ = √( 2 × D × S / H )

Where D = Annual Demand · S = Ordering Cost · H = Holding Cost per unit per year

📖 Worked Example

Scenario: An Amazon FBA seller sells 12,000 units/year of a skincare product. Each purchase order costs ₹500 to place and process. Storing one unit costs ₹120/year (warehouse + insurance + tied-up capital).

EOQ = √( 2 × 12,000 × 500 / 120 ) = √(100,000) = 316 units

✅ Place orders of 316 units — roughly 38 orders per year (every ~10 days). This minimises combined ordering + holding costs.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
What is Economic Order Quantity (EOQ)? ▾
EOQ is the ideal order quantity a company should purchase to minimise the total costs of inventory — balancing ordering frequency costs against holding/storage costs. It was developed by Ford W. Harris in 1913 and remains the most widely used inventory optimisation formula.
What should I include in ordering cost? ▾
Include: staff time to create and process the PO, supplier communication, freight/shipping setup charges, quality inspection per order, and any fixed transaction fees. A typical range is ₹200–₹2,000 per order depending on complexity.
How do I calculate holding cost? ▾
Holding cost = (warehouse rent per unit + insurance + obsolescence risk + cost of capital) per year. A common rule of thumb: holding cost ≈ 20–30% of unit cost per year. If a unit costs ₹500 and your holding rate is 24%, holding cost = ₹120/unit/year.
Does EOQ work for Amazon FBA sellers? ▾
Yes, with adjustments. For FBA, your ordering cost includes prep + shipping to FC, and your holding cost includes FBA storage fees (especially aged inventory surcharges). Also factor in lead time from your supplier to avoid stockouts before the next shipment arrives at the FC.
What are the limitations of EOQ? ▾
EOQ assumes constant demand and costs, no quantity discounts, and instant replenishment. In reality, demand fluctuates and lead times vary — which is why safety stock and reorder points are used alongside EOQ. Our AI Demand Planner handles all these variables dynamically.
